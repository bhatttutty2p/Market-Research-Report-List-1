<p><ul><li>Acid Hydrolysis Method</li><li>Enzymatic Hydrolysis Method</li><li>Electrospinning</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The nanofibrillar cellulose market encompasses various production methods, including acid hydrolysis, enzymatic hydrolysis, and electrospinning. Acid hydrolysis uses acids to break down cellulose fibers into nanofibrils, yielding high purity but requiring careful control due to the harsh conditions. Enzymatic hydrolysis employs enzymes to selectively degrade cellulose, offering a milder and more environmentally friendly approach. Electrospinning involves creating nanofibers through an electric field, producing uniform and aligned structures. Each method has unique advantages, catering to diverse applications in industries like composites, packaging, and biomedical fields.</p></p>
