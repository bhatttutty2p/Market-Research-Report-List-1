<p><p>The Knee Orthosis (KO) market comprises two main types: dynamic orthotics and static orthotics. Dynamic orthotics are designed to facilitate movement and provide support during activities, often incorporating adjustable components that allow for a range of motion while stabilizing the knee. In contrast, static orthotics offer rigid support, limiting movement to protect the knee from further injury or strain. Both types aim to enhance knee function, alleviate pain, and promote recovery in patients with various knee conditions.</p></p>
