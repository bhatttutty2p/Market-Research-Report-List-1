<p><p>The bicycle lights market is divided into two primary types: headlights and taillights. Headlights are designed to illuminate the path ahead, enhancing visibility for cyclists during nighttime or low-light conditions. They often feature various brightness levels and beam patterns. Taillights, on the other hand, focus on increasing the cyclist's visibility to others, particularly vehicles from behind. These lights typically use red LED technology and may include flashing modes to attract attention, ensuring safety on the road.</p></p>
